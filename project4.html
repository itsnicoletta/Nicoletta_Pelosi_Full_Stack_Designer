The Project Overview

This project was an exploration in art direction, guided by one of Italy's most renowned art directors and professors, Giorgio Mininno The objective was to take a generic, soulless product from the mass market and transform it into a memorable brand, utilizing various strategic approaches.

Research and Analysis

The first step, which was purely analytical, involved extensive research and observation of both direct and indirect competitors within the product’s market. This included analyzing how these brands communicated and expressed themselves on all the channels, and basing the analysis on visuals and on the golden circle.
For my project, I chose to focus on fire-starting products.

During the research phase, I noticed that most brands in this sector had similar names, were unremarkable, and offered little to no communication, with many appearing outdated and disorganized. This led me to explore related products, such as lighters and matches, where I discovered a variety of communication styles. One of my favorites was elegant minimalism, which I decided to incorporate into my brand.

Brand Development

The next crucial step was to understand the target audience, the users of the product. After numerous proposals and extensive research, I identified three key buyer personas: young individuals with a slightly hippie vibe, adventurous couples, and atmosphere enthusiasts. This insight allowed me to select a fitting brand archetype, the Lover archetype. For the brand’s name, I chose “Focum” which means fire in Latin.

The logo, inspired by the brand’s name, features a flame with a contrast recalling the typography I chose for the brand. This process required multiple iterations before finalizing the design. I also carefully selected the typography, images, and personas that would represent the brand. This phase, which spanned a month, culminated in a brand that communicates through dark, moody photography and simple, archetype-aligned copy, supported by a color palette of a few high-contrast, easily readable colors. The result was a brand that was both elegant and minimalistic, with a touch of adventure.


Communication Strategy

The final step was to determine how the brand would communicate its message. The strategy centered around using photos and videos that showcase processes like charcoal creation and wood selection, ranging from the best to the least suitable for stoves. These visuals were paired with infographics and informational carousels that explain how to use Focum products, or guide users on fire-starting techniques-targeted at individuals who enjoy the idea of the outdoors but aren’t hardcore outdoor enthusiasts.
