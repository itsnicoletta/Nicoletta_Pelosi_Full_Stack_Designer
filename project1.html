Project Overview

This project was born out of a collaboration between the Museo della Carta di Toscolano Maderno e della Valle delle Cartiere and my university, LABA. Guided by my Web Design professor, Marco Guerini, I was tasked with transforming the provided information hierarchies into a functional, visually appealing, and user-friendly website for the museum.

Client's Request

The client was eager to update the museumâ€™s website to better meet the needs of its visitors. They identified several areas for improvement, including the absence of a booking system and the need for a more organized event program. These enhancements were intended to make it easier for visitors to navigate the site and find essential information. My goal was to address these specific needs by designing a more intuitive and visually engaging user experience.

Design Process and Research

I aimed to create a clean and organized interface that would highlight the key calls to action. My design was inspired by the intricate process of paper-making and architectural sites, reflecting the precision and care that goes into this craft. To achieve a well-structured and readable layout, I chose a simple grid design. This approach helped in maintaining clarity and order, ensuring that users could easily navigate the site.


Design Solution

I opted for a minimalistic color palette with a single gradient as a visual marker, which served as a breadcrumb to help users track their location within the site. Recognizing the growing importance of mobile accessibility, I also prioritized an adaptive design. This ensured that the website was both visually appealing and fully functional across various devices, especially smartphones. My focus was on preserving a clear hierarchy of information, making sure that key content was easily accessible and prominent.
