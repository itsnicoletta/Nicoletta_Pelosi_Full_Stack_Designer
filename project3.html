The Project Overview

K-now is a user experience project focused on laying the foundational analysis required to create a digital product that is both functional and successful for users. The concept involves developing a gamified platform that connects students from various institutions participating in environmental education projects with individual citizens.
Additionally, the platform will feature a bulletin board service that aggregates events filtered by geographic area, simplifying the process of online search and registration for teachers.
Unlike similar platforms, K-now differentiates itself by combining educational and recreational elements with informative content. The platform will also feature a gamified system that rewards users for their participation in Lectures.

Research and Analysis

K-now was born out of an exploration of the challenges and pain points people face in the realm of environmental education. My team and I began by interviewing experts in the field, such as Cauto, an organization dedicated to educating young people about ecological responsibility and environmental stewardship.
Through an interview with the director of environmental education at Cauto, we mapped out the stakeholders involved in this process to understand how many entities and individuals are connected to these activities. We then identified user personas along with their respective strengths and pain points, creating a customer journey map and an empathy map for each user.
A key aspect of this process was engaging with users without preconceived notions, to truly understand what could improve their experience in this field. Problem Statements and Ideation

Based on our research, we developed problem statements for each stakeholder, considering that these issues are interconnected:
1. Theoretical lessons are often perceived as boring.
2. There are challenges with online procedures.
3. There is disorganization among staff, materials, and logistics.

To address these issues, we conducted a brainstorming session using the post-it method, where we generated three potential solutions that we later combined into one cohesive plan:
1. A multi-class platform featuring quests and games for students.
2. The use of chatbots and tutorials to assist teachers.
3. A platform where lessons and programs can be found and integrated into the school curriculum, with the ability to organize materials, associated operators, and the location of each lesson.


System Mapping and Flow Design

Following the ideation phase, we proceeded to map out the system and define the user flows within the application. This analysis served to ideate the process for interactions then advanced to the creation of the information architecture and the page flow for the UI designer to create the interface, based on high-fidelity prototypes developed by each member of the team.
This proved to be a complex application with numerous functional requirements, such as organizing human resources, managing the database, and gathering information about lessons.
Additionally, we had to consider non-functional requirements, including accessibility features due to the diverse user base the application was designed for, and environmental factors such as the distribution of devices, acknowledging that not all users may have access to the necessary technology.
